# Footage and canvas routing

## Classify on two axes

### Canvas

- Landscape: 16:9, 3:2, 4:3, ultrawide, or another horizontal ratio.
- Portrait: 9:16, 3:4, or another vertical ratio.
- Square/near-square: treat as its own canvas; do not borrow landscape spacing.

### Footage

- Talking head: one or more people are the primary evidence.
- Screen recording: software/UI is the primary evidence.
- Mixed: person and screen alternate or coexist.
- Other: product, hands-only demonstration, montage, interview, event, or generated footage.

Do not infer packaging mode from aspect ratio alone.

## Talking head

Protect:

- face, eyes, mouth, hair silhouette;
- active hands and gestures;
- gaze direction and negative space the speaker looks into;
- lower-third identity information already present.

Use:

- captions, keyword accents, lower thirds, side cards, small diagrams, B-roll or full-frame inserts at natural semantic breaks;
- punch-ins only when they do not create distracting jumps;
- graphics in genuine negative space.

Avoid:

- cards crossing the face or moving hands;
- persistent decoration around the head;
- captions covering gestures;
- full-frame replacement during emotionally important delivery unless the voiceover remains the intended focus.

## Screen recording

Protect:

- cursor target;
- buttons, checkboxes, selected state, labels, modal titles, and text that proves the claim.

Use:

- screen focus, magnifier, thin ring, short connector, compact callout;
- full-frame semantic graphics for true black/blank or abstract explanation intervals.

If semantic meaning, visible target, and cursor focus do not all match, keep raw screen plus subtitles.

## Mixed footage

Classify each segment separately. Do not reuse a talking-head safe zone after the cut changes to a screen, or a screen annotation after returning to the person.

For picture-in-picture:

- protect both rectangles;
- place graphics in remaining negative space;
- end overlays before layout or crop changes.

## Portrait layout

- Recompose hierarchy vertically.
- Prefer top hook, middle subject/evidence, bottom subtitle/action region.
- Use stacked cards and short labels rather than a compressed horizontal row.
- Increase side-safe margins for platform UI.
- Test on a mobile-sized preview.
- Keep text to fewer words and larger optical size.

## Landscape layout

- Use horizontal relationships, timelines, comparisons, and side cards when appropriate.
- Do not center everything if the subject or UI evidence already owns the center.
- Use the wider negative space without turning the frame into a slide deck.

## First two seconds

The opening needs immediate value, but evidence wins:

- if the source already shows a meaningful face, result, document, or UI, do not cover it;
- accelerate with a compact title, controlled crop, quick semantic accent, or existing motion;
- cancel the overlay when no safe placement exists.

## Full-frame inserts

Use full-frame graphics for:

- actual black/blank frames;
- abstract concepts with no useful source evidence;
- deliberate B-roll replacement;
- data, process, timeline, or comparison explanations.

When no black frames exist, full-frame inserts remain valid if the source at that moment is redundant, low-value, or explicitly approved for replacement. Record the reason and preserve the narration/audio timeline.

Do not use them by default for every spoken sentence.
