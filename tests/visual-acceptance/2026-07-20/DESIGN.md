# Visual acceptance design

## Sample 01 — portrait talking head

- Segment: 43.0–51.0 s.
- Canvas: 1080 × 1920 at 30 fps.
- Meaning: the insurer is the decisive support at the critical moment.
- Layout: small top-left chapter tag, left-edge numeric progression, bottom conclusion strip.
- Motion: tag and progression enter in 0.38–0.50 s; conclusion appears only near the spoken payoff.
- Protection: center x=300–860 and y=360–1640 is treated as the moving subject/gesture zone.

## Sample 02 — landscape screen recording

- Segment: 22.2–32.2 s.
- Canvas: 1920 × 1280 at 30 fps, derived from the native 3:2 source without aspect distortion.
- Meaning: expose the range of ready-made AI project types.
- Layout: compact top-left context label, cyan focus frame around the central evidence, bottom-right evidence chip.
- Motion: context label and focus frame settle within 0.50 s; evidence chip follows at 0.70 s.
- Protection: the central source remains visible; no opaque full-screen card is used.

## Sample 03 — mixed

- Segment: 34.5–44.5 s of screen recording plus 0.0–10.0 s of presenter footage. The endpoint stays 0.18 s before the confirmed black-screen boundary near 44.68 s.
- Canvas: 1920 × 1080 at 30 fps.
- Meaning: Codex asks questions to clarify requirements before the build route is confirmed.
- Layout: screen recording is the main stage; presenter is a 360 × 640 rounded PiP at lower left; a compact process rail occupies the upper safe zone.
- Motion: rail enters in 0.42 s; PiP settles in 0.48 s; active step changes without covering screen evidence.
- Audio: screen-recording narration only; presenter PiP is muted.
- Protection: PiP x=92–452, y=350–990; evidence focus remains at x=600–1740, y=250–850.

## Visual system

- Background overlays: navy/black with controlled transparency.
- Primary accent: `#00A9CD`.
- Secondary accents: cyan-adjacent `#5FD2E6` and cool violet `#7C5CFF`.
- Text: white and cool gray.
- No warm glow, orange fill, orange shadow or orange gradient stop.
